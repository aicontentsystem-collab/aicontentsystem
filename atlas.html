// Decision engine

How ATLAS LION decides where to trade

Every 8 seconds, ATLAS scans 7 crypto futures markets simultaneously. Each symbol is scored across 8 strategies, weighted by the self-learning engine. Only signals that pass multi-timeframe confirmation get executed. After every trade closes, the bot learns from the result and adjusts its approach.

1

Trend analysis

Checks if the market is trending using moving average crossovers (fast, medium, slow) and MACD momentum. Only trades in the direction of the dominant trend.

2

Momentum detection

Measures buying/selling pressure through RSI strength and volume surges. A sudden spike in volume with strong RSI confirms the move is real, not a fake-out.

3

Range and mean reversion

Uses Bollinger Bands to detect when price has stretched too far from its average. In sideways markets, buys at the bottom of the range and sells at the top.

4

Market sentiment

Monitors funding rates (what traders are paying to hold positions) and long/short ratios. When too many traders are on one side, ATLAS takes the opposite view — the crowd is often wrong at extremes.

5

Whale tracking

Detects large transactions ($50,000+) on Binance in real-time. Follows the "smart money" — when whales buy aggressively, ATLAS increases confidence in long signals. Mega-whale alerts ($200K+) get double weight.

6

Liquidity mapping

Analyzes the orderbook for "walls" — large buy or sell orders that act as support/resistance. Trading near these walls increases the probability of a bounce in the expected direction.

7

Reversal detection

Identifies potential turning points through RSI divergence, volume exhaustion, support/resistance bounces, double top/bottom patterns, and candlestick wick rejections (hammers and shooting stars).

8

Macro awareness

Tracks the Fear & Greed Index, Bitcoin dominance, and geopolitical risk signals. In extreme fear environments, ATLAS becomes more cautious. During geopolitical tensions (like Hormuz strait escalations), it shifts toward defensive positioning.

9

Multi-timeframe confirmation

Before executing any trade, confirms the signal on 15-minute and 1-hour charts. If the higher timeframes disagree, the signal is rejected — even if the short-term looks good. This dramatically reduces false entries.

10

Self-learning after every trade

After each trade closes (win or loss), ATLAS adjusts the weight it gives to each strategy. Strategies that are winning get more influence. On losing streaks, the bot raises its confidence threshold to become more selective. On winning streaks, it trades more aggressively.

// Risk management

Protecting capital at every step

🎯 Take profit at 10%

Every position automatically closes when it reaches +10% on the leveraged amount. This locks in gains without emotional hesitation.

🛡️ Stop loss at 6%

Maximum loss per trade is capped at -6%. The bot monitors positions every 10 seconds and closes instantly when the threshold is breached.

💰 25% compounding

Each trade uses 25% of available balance. As the account grows from $10 toward $100, position sizes automatically increase to accelerate compounding.

⏱️ 3-5 trades per hour

Rate-limited to prevent overtrading. Minimum 120s cooldown between trades on the same symbol, 45s global cooldown between any trade.

⏳ 30-minute timeout

If a position doesn't hit TP or SL within 30 minutes, it auto-closes at market price. No stale positions left open overnight.

🔄 Auto-restart

Running as a systemd service on Oracle Cloud. If the bot crashes, it automatically restarts within 15 seconds and syncs existing positions from KuCoin.
